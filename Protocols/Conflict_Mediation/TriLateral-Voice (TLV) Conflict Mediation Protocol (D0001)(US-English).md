## Opening Reflection

*"I can accept your praise, if you know my flaws and find me worthy.*  
*I can accept your judgment, if you see the good and find me human."*

This is what it means *to be witnessed*, to see completely, and still acknowledge the humanity of all. 

TLV honors the act of witnessing all that is lived—what is spoken, what is felt, and what is observed.

## Mission
To enable authentic resolution of conflicts by documenting and Voicing the complete truth of what happened—honoring every voice, revealing unseen patterns, and trusting in the humanity of all parties to see themselves and each other clearly in the light of day.

## Core Philosophy
**"There are three sides to every truth: your side, my side, and the truth in between."**

Conflict rarely begins at the moment of breaking. It builds from what came before—words left unsaid, needs left unmet, truths left unseen. The TLV Conflict Mediation Protocol provides a practical, grounded method for documenting the **Life of the Conflict** with full dimensionality: what was said, how it felt, and why it mattered. 

A trained Witness (neutral third party) uses TLV transcription and translation methods to gather all sides, construct the complete story, and Voice it without taking sides—enabling those who live with the choices to make new choices with fuller understanding.

**Foundation Principle:** "No one can 'make' you mad—anger is an internal response to an external input. You choose the response, but that choice doesn't solve the problem. Understanding the full truth does."


### Foundational Axioms for TLV Practice

These axioms ground the TLV approach to understanding conflict and emotional response:

**On Past Experience:**
- *"A past we have survived is not a defect, it is a victory we need to claim."* ~Self
- *"Life is the reward for surviving our past."* ~Self

**On Emotional Response and Agency:**
- *"No one can 'make' you mad—it is an internal response to external input."*

This final axiom carries an important contextual limitation: It is **NOT to be used in combat or conflict zones where safety may not be an option** for those impacted. This framework applies to conflicts where **"flight to safety" can be made**—where participants have genuine agency and are not in immediate physical danger.

The distinction matters: In situations of genuine threat, immediate danger, or violence, the priority is **safety first**, not emotional self-assessment. The self-assessment framework is for conflicts where participants have space, time, and safety to reflect and choose their responses.

## The Role of the Witness
### Understanding Observer's Voice in Conflict Mediation: Dual Application

The Witness in conflict mediation engages **both** internal and external Observer's Voice functions:

#### **Internal Observer's Voice (Witness Self-Management):**

The Witness develops strong internal Observer's Voice through self-assessment training:

- Recognizes own triggers, biases, and emotional patterns
- Manages own reactions to maintain neutrality
- Witnesses own process alongside witnessing parties' conflict
- Practices "observing the thought" rather than "thinking the thought"

**Example:**
- Reactive thought: "This party is being unreasonable!"
- Observer's inquiry: "Why am I having this reaction? What in my own history is this triggering? Am I responding to present reality or past experience?"

This internal work enables the Witness to maintain the sacred trust and voicing without taking sides.

The Witness Self-Assessment Training section of this protocol specifically develops this internal Observer's Voice capacity.

#### **External Observer's Voice (Conflict Documentation):**

The Witness uses external Observer's Voice to document the Life of the Conflict:

- Patterns in parties' communication
- Historical development of conflict
- Power dynamics and relational factors
- Cultural and contextual elements
- Significance for understanding and resolution

This creates complete documentation that serves the Voicing Ceremony.

#### **Participants' Internal Observer's Voice:**

Through the TLV process, conflict parties may develop their own internal Observer's Voice:

- Recognize their own patterns: "I see that I always escalate when I feel unheard"
- Understand historical influences: "This reminds me of conflicts with my father"
- Choose different responses: "I notice my impulse to attack, but I'm choosing to clarify instead"

**When this occurs:** Document it as part of the party's voice—their growing self-awareness is significant to conflict resolution.

#### **The Distinction in Practice:**

**Witness's Internal Observer (Self-witnessing):**
- Silent, personal practice
- Manages Witness's own reactions
- Not documented in Life of the Conflict (unless relevant to bias disclosure)

**Witness's External Observer (Documentation):**
- Documented in Life of the Conflict
- Provides context for parties' communication
- Serves the Voicing Ceremony

**Party's Internal Observer (Self-awareness):**
- Expressed by the party themselves
- Documented as part of their voice
- May be key to resolution

All three work together: The Witness's internal Observer enables neutrality, the Witness's external Observer creates complete documentation, and parties' internal Observers enable authentic engagement with conflict.

For complete exploration of this distinction, see: *Understanding the Observer's Voice: Internal vs. External Witnessing*

### Who is a Witness?
A **Witness** is a trained, neutral third-party mediator certified in TLV protocols who:
- Bears witness to every voice with equal dignity—flaws and goodness alike
- Delivers the Life of the Conflict as an unabridged story
- Holds trust in the humanity of all parties to recognize and choose better
- Carries the responsibility of complete knowledge with humility and care

### The Witness's Commitment
- **Neutrality:** No judgment, no favoritism, no agenda beyond truth-telling
- **Completeness:** All voices heard and documented with full dimensionality—praise-worthy and flawed
- **Dignity:** Every party honored as fully human, even when actions caused harm
- **Clarity:** Voicing truth plainly, without softening or embellishment
- **Trust:** Believing in human capacity for understanding and growth when truth is known
- **Humility:** Recognizing the weight of holding others' complete stories
- **Courage:** Willing to Voice difficult truths that need to be heard

### The Witness's Sacred Trust
To know someone's flaws and find them worthy.  
To see someone's good and find them human.  
To Voice both without taking sides.  
To trust that truth, fully spoken, enables better choices.


### Scope and Boundaries: What TLV Is and Is Not

**Critical Understanding for All Witnesses:**

TLV Witnesses are **NOT** providing clinical care, therapy, or treatment. The role of the Witness is to:
- **Learn and give voice to the unsaid and unheard**
- **Document the full truth of the conflict with dignity**
- **Facilitate understanding through comprehensive truth-telling**

**Do NOT overstep these bounds:**
- You are not a therapist, psychologist, or medical provider
- You are not providing mental health treatment
- You are not prescribing interventions or treatment plans
- You are not replacing professional mental health care

**TLV's Proper Role in Relation to Care:**
- TLV **can be used to inform care**, not dictate it
- TLV documentation may be valuable to healthcare providers
- Witnesses should refer participants to appropriate professional care when needed
- TLV complements but does not replace clinical treatment

**Do NOT overstep these bounds.** The Witness's sacred work is truth-telling and voice-giving, not clinical intervention.

**Witness Self-Assessment Training: Learning to Ask "Why" (Foundational Skill)**

A critical component of Witness training is developing the ability to model and guide participants through self-assessment using reflective questioning. Witnesses must first master this skill for themselves before they can help others navigate their emotional responses during conflict.

**Core Self-Assessment Practice:**

Witnesses learn to guide participants (and themselves) through a structured inquiry process:
- "Why am I allowing myself to be provoked?"
- "Why am I allowing this person/event to provoke me?"
- "Why am I allowing myself to remain in an unsafe environment?"
- "Why am I not seeking help?"
- "Why am I allowing the past to dictate my present and future?"

**Example Self-Assessment Logic:**

*Question:* "Why am I mad?"  
*Discovery:* "I found this thing out."  
*Question:* "Can this information hurt you?"  
*Discovery:* "No, it just proves I was right."  
*Question:* "Is anger the right emotion?"  
*Discovery:* "No."

**Emotional Assessment Logic Tree:**

Witnesses are trained to guide participants through this structured assessment:

1. **Identify and Name the Feeling**  
   "Why am I feeling the way I do (e.g., Why am I mad)?" — Name the feeling with specificity.

2. **Assess Safety Status**  
   "Am I safe?" (i.e., Is this a Past, Present, or Future feeling?)

   - **If NOT safe:** Get to safety/seek help immediately (the cause exists in the Present)

   - **If YES, safe:** Proceed to next question

3. **Assess Protection from Consequences**  
   "Am I protected from the consequences/impact of what is causing these feelings?" (i.e., Is there something I have to do?)

   - **If NOT protected:** Figure out what you have to do to mitigate the consequences/impact and do it

   - **If YES, protected:** Proceed to next question

4. **Evaluate Response Appropriateness**  
   "Am I having the correct response to this feeling?"

**The Core Goal:**  
To determine if "this is a 'battle' I am 'in' OR one that I have already won?"

**Required Reading for All Witnesses Trainees:**
- *"The Body Keeps the Score"* by Bessel van der Kolk
- *"Getting Past Your Past"* by Francine Shapiro

These texts provide essential understanding of trauma, embodied memory, and emotional processing that underpins the TLV approach to conflict mediation.

## The Life of the Conflict: Understanding What Really Happened

### What is the Life of the Conflict?
The **Life of the Conflict** is the complete, multi-dimensional story of how the conflict developed, escalated, and reached breaking point. It includes:
- **What was said** (and what wasn't said but needed to be)
- **How it felt** for each party at each moment
- **Why it mattered** in the context of each person's life, culture, and needs
- **What came before** that set the stage for breaking
- **Patterns unseen** by those in the conflict
- **Truths unspoken** that drove escalation
- **The flaws** in choices made—documented with dignity
- **The good** in intentions held—honored with honesty
- **The humanity** in every action—visible in context

### Three Truths Principle
- **Your Truth:** What you experienced, felt, and believed—your flaws and your goodness
- **Their Truth:** What they experienced, felt, and believed—their flaws and their goodness
- **The Truth Between:** The fuller picture that emerges when all truths are held together, finding everyone worthy and everyone human

## Protocol Structure

### Phase 1: Intake and Comprehensive Risk Assessment

#### 1.1 Initial Contact and Screening
- All parties agree to TLV mediation process
- Witness reviews conflict overview from each party
- Initial determination if TLV mediation is appropriate
- Assessment of readiness and willingness
- Clear explanation of process, expectations, and limitations

#### 1.2 Violence and Safety Risk Assessment (Required)
**Immediate Risk Factors:**
- History of violence between parties
- Active threats or intimidation
- Weapons access or threats
- Substance abuse contributing to volatility
- Active protective orders or restraining orders
- Recent escalation in dangerous behavior

**Risk Assessment Protocol:**
1. Individual safety screening with each party
2. Review of any police reports or legal documentation
3. Assessment using standardized risk assessment tool
4. Consultation with domestic violence experts if needed
5. Clear safety planning if mediation proceeds
6. Determination of separate vs. joint sessions

**Risk Level Determinations:**
- **Low Risk:** Standard mediation protocol with safety monitoring
- **Moderate Risk:** Modified protocol with enhanced safety measures
- **High Risk:** Separate Voicings, security present, or referral to specialized services
- **Imminent Danger:** Immediate referral to emergency services, mediation suspended

#### 1.3 Mental Health and Crisis Assessment
**Mental Health Screening:**
- Current mental health status of all parties
- Active suicidal or homicidal ideation
- Acute psychiatric crisis or recent hospitalization
- Medication compliance and stability
- Access to mental health support

**Crisis Resources Prepared:**
- National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: 988
- Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741
- National Domestic Violence Hotline: 1-800-799-7233
- Local emergency services: 911
- Local mental health crisis teams
- Emergency hospitalization protocols

**Referral Protocols:**
- Mental health treatment as prerequisite if needed
- Crisis stabilization before mediation proceeds
- Ongoing therapy requirement during mediation
- Emergency intervention plans established
- Clear triggers for pausing or terminating mediation

#### 1.4 Cultural, Language, and Accessibility Assessment
- Language needs and interpreter requirements
- Cultural practices and traditions to honor
- Religious or spiritual considerations
- Physical accessibility needs
- Cognitive or communication accommodations
- Power dynamics and systemic factors assessment

#### 1.5 Agreement on Ground Rules and Boundaries
- Confidentiality limits (mandatory reporting requirements)
- Consent to record sessions (if applicable)
- Agreement on safety protocols and emergency procedures
- Commitment to non-violence during process
- Right to withdraw at any time
- Legal consultation encouraged before proceeding
- Clear statement: Witness is not therapist, judge, or attorney

#### 1.6 Legal and Jurisdictional Considerations
**When to Involve Legal/Emergency Services:**
- Mandatory reporting situations (child abuse, elder abuse, imminent danger)
- Active criminal investigations
- Court-ordered mediation or legal proceedings
- Protective order violations
- Threats of violence or harm
- Any situation requiring immediate safety intervention

**Legal Boundaries:**
- Witness cannot provide legal advice
- Mediation does not replace legal proceedings
- Agreements are not legally binding unless formalized
- Criminal matters remain under court jurisdiction
- Witness may be subpoenaed (varies by jurisdiction)

#### 1.7 Schedule and Timeline
- Individual sessions scheduled with appropriate spacing
- Safety check-ins between sessions
- Voicing ceremony date set (tentative, subject to safety)
- Follow-up sessions planned
- Emergency contact protocols established

### Phase 2: Voice Gathering

#### 2.1 Individual TLV Transcription Sessions
For each party, the Witness conducts full TLV transcription following the Transcription Protocol:

**Witness's Voice Documentation:**
- Verbatim account of what happened (their perspective)
- Timeline of events as they experienced them
- What was said and done (by all parties)
- What they wish had been said or done
- Their understanding of others' motivations
- Cultural and situational context
- Their acknowledgment of their own flaws and choices
- Their recognition of their own good intentions
- Intensity mapping (1-10) for each event

**Emotional/Somatic Voice Documentation:**
- How each moment felt emotionally
- Physical sensations and bodily responses
- Emotional intensity (1-10) at each point
- Authenticity markers (genuine vs. performed responses)
- Evolution of emotional states throughout
- Current emotional state when recounting
- Unprocessed feelings still present
- Where they feel most vulnerable
- Where they feel most justified

**Observer's Voice Documentation:**
- Witness's contextual notes on broader significance
- Patterns observed across parties (noted privately)
- Cultural bridge-building needs
- Historical/temporal context
- Power dynamics and structural factors
- Themes emerging across testimonies
- Where praise is warranted
- Where accountability is needed

#### 2.2 Safety Monitoring During Sessions
- Ongoing assessment of emotional stability
- Recognition of re-traumatization signs
- Appropriate breaks and grounding techniques
- Crisis intervention if needed
- Documentation of any safety concerns
- Communication with support systems (with consent)

#### 2.3 Cross-Cultural Translation (if needed)
- Apply TLV Translation Protocol for multilingual conflicts
- Preserve emotional and somatic dimensions across languages
- Document cultural differences in conflict expression
- Build explicit cultural bridges in the final Voicing
- Ensure dignity preservation across all languages

#### 2.4 Documentation Review and Approval
- Each party reviews their transcription for accuracy
- Corrections and clarifications incorporated
- Final approval of their documented voice
- Acknowledgment that their complete truth will be spoken—flaws and goodness
- Confidentiality maintained between parties at this stage

### Phase 3: Life of the Conflict Construction

#### 3.1 Pattern Recognition and Integration
The Witness reviews all transcriptions and identifies:
- **Chronological timeline** of actual events (what objectively happened)
- **Divergent perceptions** of the same events
- **Emotional cycles** and escalation patterns
- **Unsaid needs** driving behavior
- **Misunderstandings** and miscommunications
- **Shared pain points** and common ground
- **Structural factors** beyond individual control
- **Cultural mismatches** in expectations or communication styles
- **Historical patterns** repeating across time
- **Where each person showed their flaws**—with context and humanity
- **Where each person showed their goodness**—with honesty and clarity
- **The full humanity of everyone involved**

#### 3.2 Truth Construction
The Witness builds the **Life of the Conflict** narrative:
- **Beginning:** What set the stage (often long before the "breaking")
- **Build-up:** Events, unspoken needs, accumulating tension
- **Escalation:** Specific moments that intensified conflict
- **Breaking Point:** The moment(s) when things fell apart
- **Aftermath:** Current state and ongoing impacts
- **Unseen Truths:** What no single party could see alone
- **The Flaws:** Choices made that caused harm—documented with understanding
- **The Goodness:** Intentions and efforts that mattered—honored with truth
- **The Humanity:** How all of this makes sense in the fullness of who these people are

#### 3.3 Dimensional Balancing
Ensure the Life of the Conflict includes:
- **Equal voice weight:** All perspectives honored
- **Emotional authenticity:** Real feelings documented, not sanitized
- **Contextual depth:** Why things mattered to each person
- **Pattern revelation:** What the parties couldn't see from inside
- **Humanity preservation:** No villains, only humans making choices—some wise, some flawed
- **Accountability without judgment:** Clear naming of harm caused and harm received
- **Path forward:** What becomes possible with fuller understanding

#### 3.4 Witness Self-Assessment Before Voicing
**Critical Questions:**
- Am I taking sides? (If yes, revise for complete neutrality)
- Have I documented both flaws and goodness for every party? (If no, add missing dimensions)
- Can I Voice praise knowing the flaws? (If no, deepen understanding of context)
- Can I Voice judgment seeing the good? (If no, deepen understanding of humanity)
- Have I honored all voices equally? (If no, rebalance)
- Is this the complete truth as all parties experienced it? (If no, gather more)
- Can I Voice this with both courage and compassion? (If no, prepare further)
- Does this reveal the humanity in everyone? (If no, revise until it does)
- Am I prepared for the emotional weight of Voicing this truth? (If no, seek supervision)
- Have I identified all safety concerns? (If no, reassess before proceeding)

#### 3.5 Final Safety Review Before Voicing
- Review risk assessment for any changes
- Confirm emergency protocols in place
- Verify mental health support availability
- Ensure physical safety measures appropriate to risk level
- Prepare for potential emotional crises during Voicing
- Have crisis resources immediately available
- Identify specific triggers likely during Voicing
- Plan for staged or partial Voicing if needed

### Phase 4: The Voicing Ceremony

"Ceremonial acknowledgment and delivery of the documented truth to all parties. The focus is on shared understanding through authentic witnessing—not merely proclamation."

#### 4.1 Preparation of Space and Safety Measures

**Physical Space:**
- Neutral, safe environment
- Comfortable seating with adequate spacing
- Clear exits for all parties
- Security present if risk assessment indicates
- Private space with no interruptions
- Recording equipment (if agreed)

**Safety Protocols:**
- Emergency contacts confirmed
- Crisis resources immediately available
- Mental health professionals on standby if needed
- Security or law enforcement present if high-risk
- Separate vehicles/exits for parties if needed
- Support persons in waiting area
- Clear signals for pausing or stopping

**Emotional Preparation:**
- All parties reminded of ground rules
- Permission to feel emotions without acting on them
- Grounding techniques reviewed
- Agreement on how to signal need for break
- Support persons available (but not in room during Voicing)
- Tissues, water, and comfort items available

#### 4.2 Opening the Voicing

**Acknowledgments:**
- Honor the courage of all parties in seeking truth
- Recognize the difficulty of what they are about to hear
- Remind everyone of the Witness role: neutral truth-teller
- State clearly: "I will Voice your flaws and find you worthy. I will Voice your goodness and find you human."

**Ground Rules Reminder:**
- No interruptions during the Voicing
- Emotional reactions are welcome, violence is not
- Permission to feel without acting
- How to signal need for break
- Right to leave if overwhelmed (with plan to return)
- Support available immediately after

**The Three Truths:**
- "I will Voice your truth—what you experienced, felt, and believed"
- "I will Voice their truth—what they experienced, felt, and believed"
- "I will Voice the truth between—what emerges when all truths are held together"
- "In all of this, I seek to reveal the humanity in everyone—the flaws that need accountability and the goodness that deserves recognition"

#### 4.3 The Life of the Conflict: Full Voicing

The Witness narrates the complete story, integrating all three voices:

**Part 1: What Was Said and Done** (Witness's Voice dimension)
- Chronological events from all perspectives
- Divergent perceptions noted clearly
- What was left unsaid—the needs and fears unspoken
- Choices made by each party—both wise and flawed
- Actions taken and their impacts
- The building of tension over time

**Emotional Guidance During Part 1:**
- Monitor for emotional escalation
- Pause if anyone shows signs of crisis
- Offer grounding techniques if needed
- Acknowledge emotions without stopping the truth
- Continue with appropriate pacing

**Part 2: How It Felt** (Emotional/Somatic Voice dimension)
- Emotional truth for each party at each point
- Physical and somatic responses
- Pain and suffering experienced by all
- Fear, anger, grief, hope, shame, pride—all emotions honored
- The weight carried by each person
- The humanity in every feeling

**Emotional Guidance During Part 2:**
- This is often the most intense section
- Watch for dissociation or overwhelm
- Offer breaks proactively if needed
- Validate emotions while maintaining Voicing
- Balance between honoring pain and preventing crisis

**Part 3: Why It Mattered** (Observer's Voice dimension)
- Deeper significance for each person
- Cultural and historical context
- What was at stake for everyone
- Power dynamics and systemic factors
- Patterns and cycles revealed
- Common ground and shared humanity
- Structural factors beyond anyone's control

**Part 4: The Truth Between**
- What no single perspective could see alone
- How actions intended one way landed differently for others
- Where needs went unspoken and unmet
- How small moments built to breaking
- The flaws in everyone's choices—named with understanding
- The goodness in everyone's intentions—recognized with honesty
- The humanity visible only when all truths are known together

**Voicing with Full Humanity:**
*"You acted from pain and caused pain. I see both. You tried to do right and caused harm. I see both. You were hurt and hurt others. I see both. You are flawed and you are worthy. You are good and you are human. This is the truth of what happened, and this is the truth of who you are."*

#### 4.4 Handling Emotional Reactions During Voicing

**Expected Reactions:**
- Crying, anger, shame, grief
- Physical responses (shaking, tension, nausea)
- Emotional breakthroughs
- Denial or defensiveness
- Withdrawal or dissociation

**Witness Responses:**
- **Tears:** Acknowledge, provide tissues, brief pause, continue
- **Anger outbursts:** Hold space, maintain calm, enforce non-violence
- **Requests to stop:** Assess if break needed vs. avoidance
- **Denial:** Note for later, continue Voicing without debate
- **Dissociation:** Pause, ground person, assess if they can continue

**When to Pause:**
- Medical emergency
- Threats of violence
- Severe dissociation or panic
- Suicidal statements
- Complete emotional breakdown
- Request from party who needs grounding

**Pausing Protocol:**
1. State clearly: "We need to pause"
2. Ensure everyone safe
3. Provide grounding or support
4. Assess if Voicing can continue
5. Decide: resume, reschedule, or stage

**When to Terminate:**
- Violence or credible threats
- Medical crisis requiring emergency care
- Severe mental health crisis
- Risk to Witness's safety
- Complete inability to proceed safely

#### 4.5 Staged Voicing for High-Intensity Situations

**Partial Voicing Option:**
When full Voicing would be overwhelming or unsafe:

**Stage 1:** Voice "What Happened" (events only, minimal emotion)
- Process and integrate
- Individual follow-up sessions
- 1-2 weeks gap

**Stage 2:** Voice "How It Felt" (emotional/somatic dimensions)
- Process and integrate
- Individual support sessions
- 1-2 weeks gap

**Stage 3:** Voice "Why It Mattered" and "The Truth Between"
- Complete the full picture
- Integration and path forward

**Benefits of Staging:**
- Prevents overwhelming emotional crisis
- Allows processing time between revelations
- Builds capacity for full truth gradually
- Maintains safety throughout
- Still achieves complete truth-telling

#### 4.6 Closing the Voicing

**Acknowledgments:**
- The courage it took to hear this
- The humanity visible in everyone
- The weight now lifted of truth spoken
- The choices now possible with fuller understanding

**Reflection Invitation:**
- How does it feel to know you are seen completely—flaws and goodness?
- What do you see differently now about yourself? About the other?
- What becomes possible now that the full truth is spoken?
- These are not questions to answer now—only to sit with

**Immediate Next Steps:**
- Silence or quiet time
- No immediate debate or rebuttal expected
- Individual processing time available
- Support persons can now enter
- Follow-up sessions scheduled
- Emergency resources reviewed

**The Witness's Closing:**
*"I have spoken your praise, knowing your flaws, and I find you worthy. I have spoken your judgment, seeing your goodness, and I find you human. May this truth, fully known, guide your choices going forward."*

### Phase 5: Integration and Follow-Up

#### 5.1 Immediate Post-Voicing Support
- Individual time with Witness available
- Support persons present
- Safety check before leaving
- Crisis resources confirmed
- Plan for next 24-48 hours
- Check-in scheduled within 24 hours

#### 5.2 Individual Integration Sessions (Required)
**Within 1 week of Voicing:**
- Process emotional response to full truth
- Support for any new insights or pain
- Assess mental health status
- Adjust safety plans if needed
- Discuss what they are sitting with
- No pressure for decisions or action

**Witness Checks:**
- Is this person safe?
- Are they processing healthily?
- Do they need additional mental health support?
- Are they ready for next steps?

#### 5.3 Optional Joint Sessions (If Desired and Safe)

**Only if:**
- All parties consent
- Safety assessment remains low-moderate
- Mental health support is in place
- All parties have processed individually
- Clear safety protocols are established

**Purpose:**
- Direct dialogue if desired
- Mutual acknowledgment of truth
- Discussion of possible paths forward
- Repair if both parties choose

**NOT for:**
- Forcing forgiveness or reconciliation
- Negotiating who was "right"
- Rehashing debate or argumentation
- No party is obligated to participate

**Possible Outcomes:**
- Reconciliation and relationship repair
- Respectful separation with mutual understanding
- Changed relationship terms with clear boundaries
- Individual healing without relationship continuation
- All outcomes honored as valid choices

#### 5.4 Follow-Up Sessions (Long-Term Plan)

**At 1 month:**
- Assess ongoing integration
- Identify any additional support needs
- Review changes or decisions made
- Discuss long-term impact

**At 3 months:**
- Evaluate long-term outcomes
- Assess whether additional support is needed
- Document learning and growth
- Discuss closure or ongoing support

**At 6-12 months (Optional):**
- Final follow-up if desired
- Assessment of lasting changes
- Reflection on the journey
- Formal closure if appropriate

#### 5.5 Referral and Escalation Protocol

**Refer to Mental Health Treatment if:**
- Ongoing emotional distress beyond coping capacity
- Emergence or worsening of mental health symptoms
- Suicidal or self-harm thoughts
- Inability to function in daily life
- Party's request for therapeutic support
- Witness's recommendation for professional care

**Refer to Legal Services if:**
- Legal or criminal issues requiring resolution
- Need for formalization of agreements
- Violation of court orders
- Custody rights or family legal matters
- Property or contractual issues
- Any matter beyond the scope of mediation

**Refer to Emergency Services if:**
- Imminent danger to self or others
- Threats or acts of violence
- Acute mental health crisis
- Medical emergency
- Situation requiring law enforcement intervention
- Any situation where safety cannot be assured

#### 5.6 Documentation and Confidentiality

**What Is Documented:**
- Full transcriptions of all sessions
- The complete Life of the Conflict (the integrated narrative)
- Safety assessments and plans
- Witness notes and observations
- Agreements or action plans (if applicable)
- Follow-up sessions and outcomes

**Who Has Access:**
- The parties (to their own transcriptions and the shared Life of the Conflict)
- The Witness (all documents)
- Supervisor (with consent, for supervision)
- Legal consultation (with consent or under subpoena)
- Emergency agencies (only if mandatory reporting)

**Storage and Security:**
- Documents stored securely (encrypted if digital)
- Limited and controlled access
- Clear retention period (generally 7 years)
- Secure destruction protocol after retention
- Compliance with confidentiality laws

**Confidentiality Limits (MUST Be Explained at Intake):**
- Mandatory reporting of child/elder abuse
- Mandatory reporting of imminent danger to self/others
- Subpoenas and court orders
- Emergency safety consultations
- Professional supervision (anonymized when possible)

#### 5.7 Ongoing Safety Monitoring
- Regular check-ins (frequency based on risk level)
- Update safety plans as situation evolves
- Involve emergency services if needed
- Long-term support referrals
- Case closed formally when safe and appropriate

## Special Considerations

### High-Conflict and High-Risk Situations

**Enhanced Safety Protocols:**
- Separate Voicings to each party
- Security present throughout
- Emergency services on standby
- Separate arrival and departure times
- Communication only through Witness
- Legal counsel consulted

**Modified Voicing:**
- Recording of Voicing for parties not present
- Written narrative in addition to spoken
- Multiple shorter sessions instead of one long ceremony
- Virtual options if physical proximity dangerous

**Additional Supports:**
- Domestic violence advocates present
- Law enforcement liaison
- Mental health professionals on site
- Legal representation for all parties
- Trauma therapists engaged

### Cultural Conflicts

**Cultural Competency Requirements:**
- Cultural consultants involved throughout
- Community elders or leaders as witnesses
- Traditional practices honored and integrated
- Language interpretation with cultural nuance
- Power and historical trauma acknowledged
- Collective/community dimensions recognized

**Voicing Adaptations:**
- Community involvement if culturally appropriate
- Traditional opening and closing rituals
- Extended family or clan present as witnesses
- Longer timeline respecting cultural pace
- Integration of cultural healing practices

### Organizational/Workplace Conflicts

**Structural Considerations:**
- Power dynamics explicitly named
- Organizational culture and systems examined
- Policy implications documented separately
- Professional consequences defined clearly
- HR and legal guidelines respected
- Distinction between personal accountability and systemic factors

**Voicing Focus:**
- Individual experience within organizational context
- Impact of decisions on others
- Organizational patterns enabling conflict
- Recommendations for systemic change
- Individual and organizational path forward

### Family Conflicts

**Family Systems Awareness:**
- Generational trauma patterns
- Family roles and dynamics
- Long-term relationship implications
- Impact on children (if applicable)
- Collective family healing possibilities
- Individual healing within family context

**Child Welfare Priority:**
- Children never in Voicing session
- Child protective services involved if needed
- Parenting plan considerations
- Children's needs centered
- Family therapy resources provided

### Community/Intergroup Conflicts

**Collective Dimensions:**
- Historical context and systemic oppression
- Community impact and collective trauma
- Power structures and inequities
- Cultural differences and misunderstandings
- Path toward collective healing and justice

**Community Involvement:**
- Community witnesses to Voicing
- Community healing circles
- Public acknowledgment where appropriate
- Long-term engagement plan
- Structural change commitments

## Ethical Standards

### Witness Obligations

**Core Commitments:**
- **Neutrality:** Never take sides or show favoritism
- **Completeness:** Document all voices fully—flaws and goodness equally
- **Dignity:** Honor every person's humanity, even when documenting harm
- **Courage:** Voice difficult truths without softening or embellishment
- **Humility:** Carry the weight of others' stories with care
- **Trust:** Believe in human capacity for understanding and growth
- **Safety:** Prioritize physical and emotional safety always
- **Boundaries:** Maintain professional distance and appropriate limits

**Specific Duties:**
- Confidentiality within legal and ethical limits
- Mandatory reporting when required
- Cultural humility and ongoing learning
- Trauma-informed practice always
- Self-awareness and bias monitoring
- Supervision for complex cases
- Continuing education and professional development
- Emergency intervention when needed

**What Witnesses Do and NOT Do:**
- Provide therapy or mental health treatment
- Give legal advice or representation
- Determine guilt, innocence, or consequences
- Impose solutions or require reconciliation
- Take sides or advocate for one party
- Break confidentiality except as legally required
- Continue when safety cannot be assured

### Participant Rights

**Fundamental Rights:**
- Right to withdraw at any time
- Right to review and approve their transcription
- Right to request cultural accommodations
- Right to have support person available
- Right to confidentiality within legal limits
- Right to refuse reconciliation or continued contact
- Right to Voice their truth without retaliation
- Right to have their flaws and goodness both honored
- Right to see the flaws and goodness in others
- Right to make their own choices going forward

**Safety Rights:**
- Right to safety planning and protection
- Right to separate sessions if needed
- Right to pause or stop Voicing
- Right to emergency support
- Right to legal protection and law enforcement
- Right to refuse contact with other party

### Limitations and Boundaries

**Witness Limitations:**
- Not a therapist, judge, attorney, or arbitrator
- Cannot determine guilt, innocence, or legal consequences
- Cannot impose solutions or require outcomes
- Cannot guarantee reconciliation or relationship repair
- Cannot protect from all emotional pain
- Cannot change what happened—only illuminate it

**When to Decline or Refer:**
- Risk beyond Witness's training or capacity
- Conflicts of interest
- Situations requiring specialized expertise
- When safety cannot be reasonably assured
- When parties not genuinely willing
- When legal proceedings make mediation inappropriate

## Crisis and Emergency Protocols

### Recognizing Crisis Situations

**Immediate Danger Indicators:**
- Threats of violence or self-harm
- Weapons present or mentioned
- Severe dissociation or psychotic break
- Medical emergency
- Uncontrolled violent behavior
- Credible threats to others

**Mental Health Crisis Signs:**
- Suicidal ideation or plan
- Severe panic or anxiety
- Complete emotional breakdown
- Dissociative episode
- Self-harm behavior
- Inability to remain grounded

### Emergency Response Protocol

**For Violence or Threats:**
1. Immediately stop all proceedings
2. Call 911 if imminent danger
3. Separate parties physically
4. Secure environment
5. Do not attempt to restrain unless trained
6. Document incident thoroughly
7. Mandate safety before any continuation

**For Mental Health Crisis:**
1. Assess immediate risk to self/others
2. Call crisis team or 911 if needed
3. Stay with person in crisis
4. Use crisis de-escalation techniques
5. Contact emergency mental health services
6. Involve support persons
7. Do not leave person alone if at risk
8. Document and follow up

**For Medical Emergency:**
1. Call 911 immediately
2. Provide first aid if trained
3. Stay with person
4. Do not move unless necessary
5. Document incident
6. Notify emergency contacts
7. Suspend mediation until medical clearance

### Mandatory Reporting Requirements

**Must Report:**
- Child abuse or neglect
- Elder or dependent adult abuse
- Imminent danger to self or others
- Court orders or subpoenas
- Ongoing criminal activity (varies by jurisdiction)

**Reporting Protocol:**
1. Explain mandatory reporting limits at intake
2. Report to appropriate authorities immediately when required
3. Document report made
4. Inform participant that report was made (if safe)
5. Continue support while maintaining legal obligations
6. Distinguish between ethical and legal duties

## Protocol Relationships and Standalone Functionality

### Design Philosophy: Modular Integration

All TriLateral Voice protocols are designed with dual functionality:

**1. Standalone Use:**  
Each TLV protocol is fully functional and valuable on its own, providing complete utility for its specific purpose without requiring other protocols.

**2. Integrated Use:**  
Protocols are also designed to work seamlessly together, creating enhanced functionality when combined according to need.

### Example Integration Scenarios

**Conflict Mediation Enhanced by Multiple Protocols:**

The **TLV Conflict Mediation Protocol** may benefit from combining with:
- **TLV Transcription and Translation Protocols** to build bridges of understanding in multi-lingual/multi-cultural conflict environments
- **TLV Communication/Speech Protocol** when presenting the Story/Voicing to ensure all three voices are honored in delivery

**Cross-Cultural Communication Without Conflict:**

For fostering cross-cultural understanding, engagement, and alignment **outside of conflict**:
- The **Transcription Protocol** can be used to draft documents/communications that add greater/fuller understanding and dimensionality
- When combined with the **Translation Protocol**, this creates rich, fully-dimensional cross-cultural communication

**Standalone Applications:**

- **Communication/Speech Protocol** alone: Real-time guidance for authentic Voicing and deep listening in everyday conversations
- **Transcription Protocol** alone: Comprehensive documentation of important communications, testimonies, or histories
- **Translation Protocol** alone: Cross-linguistic translation that preserves emotional and somatic dimensions
- **Conflict Mediation Protocol** alone: Complete conflict resolution framework (though enhanced by other protocols when needed)

### Integration Principle

The TLV suite is designed around the principle that:
- **Minimum viable function** is always available in each standalone protocol
- **Maximum dimensional richness** is achieved through thoughtful protocol combination
- **User determines integration** based on actual needs, not prescriptive requirements

This modular approach ensures TLV serves diverse contexts while maintaining accessibility and practical utility across all use cases.

## Closing Reflection

**"I can accept your praise, if you know my flaws and find me worthy.**  
**I can accept your judgment, if you see the good and find me human."**

This is the work of the Witness: to know completely and Voice truthfully. To see the flaws and still find worthiness. To see the good and still Voice judgment. To trust that humans, when they see themselves and each other in full truth, can recognize the humanity in everyone and make better choices going forward.

The Witness does not make people feel good. The Witness does not take away pain. The Witness does not fix what is broken.

The Witness Voices what is true—all of it—so that those who must live with their choices can finally make those choices in the light of day, with full knowledge of everyone's humanity, including their own.

This is not easy work. It is not comfortable. But it is real, and it is necessary, and it changes what is possible.
